\ques{1}{b}{10} Let X be a continuous random variable with the probability density function \[ f(x) = \begin{cases} 2/x^2, \quad &\text{if } 1<x<2 \\ 0,  & \text{elsewhere} \end{cases}\] Find the cumulative distribution function and the probability density function of $Y=X^2$.

\ques{3}{a}{20} $X_1$ is the time that a customer takes from getting on line at a service desk in a bank to completion of a service and $X_2$ is the time to wait in line before reaching the service desk $(X_1 \geq X_2)$. The joint probbaility density funciton of $(X_1, X_2$ is given by \[ f(x_1,x_2) = \begin{cases} e^{-x_1},  & 0\leq x_2\leq x_1 < \infty  \\ 0, &\text{elsewhere} \end{cases}\] Let $Y_1 = X_1 + X_2$ and $Y_2 = X_1 - X_2$. Find the joint probability distribution function of $Y_1$ and $Y_2$. Are $Y_1$ and $Y_2$ independently distributed? Give reason.
