\ques{1}{b}{10} Define convergence in distribution and in mean. Let $\{X_n\}_{n=1}^{\infty}$ be a sequence of independent random variables such that \[ \begin{aligned}
    X_n &= n^3 \text{ with probability }\dfrac{1}{n^2} \\
    &= 0 \text{ with probability }1 - \dfrac{1}{n^2}
\end{aligned} \] Show that $X_n \rightarrow 0$ in distribution.
