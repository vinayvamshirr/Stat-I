\ques{1}{c}{12} Examine whether the central limit theorem holds for the sequence $\{ X_n, n \geq 1\}$ of random variables with
\[ \begin{aligned}
   P (X_n = n) &= P(X_n= -n) = \dfrac{1}{2n^3} \\
P(X_n =0) &= 1 - \frac{1}{n^3}, \enskip n\geq1. 
\end{aligned} \]
\ques{2}{c}{20} Show that for a sequence of random variables $\{X_n\}$, convergence in probability implies convergence in distribution. Is the converse true? Substantiate your answer.
