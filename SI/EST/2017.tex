\ques{1}{d}{10} Let $x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_n$ be $n$ observations, each independently uniformly distributed between $\theta - \dfrac{1}{2}$ and $\theta + \dfrac{1}{2}$. Find the general form of the maximum likelihood estimator of $\theta$. Verify that \[\dfrac{2Y_1 + 4Y_n - 1}{6} \text{  and  } \dfrac{4Y_1 + 2Y_n + 1}{6} \] are both MLEs, where $Y_1 = Min(X_1,\ldots,X_n)$ and $Y_n = Max(X_1,\ldots,X_n)$.

\ques{1}{e}{10} Let $X_1,X_2,\ldots,X_n$ be a random sample from uniform population over $(0,\theta)$ and let $M = Max(X_1,X_2,\ldots,X_n)$. Show that $M$ is a biased but consistent estimator of $\theta$.

\ques{2}{a}{20} Let $X_1,X_2,\ldots,X_n$ be independently and identically distributed observations of a distribution with probability density function $f_{\theta}(x)$. If $Y=g(X_1,X_2,\ldots,X_n)$ is an unbiased estimate of $theta$, then
\[var_{\theta}(Y) \geq \dfrac{1}{nE_{\theta}\left[\left(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial \theta}\log f_{\theta}(X) \right)^2\right]} \] Use this inequality to show that the sample mean is a uniformly minimum variance unbiased estimator of mean of a Poisson distribution.

\[ f(x) = \theta x^{\theta -1}, 0 <x<1, \theta > 0\] Find the maximum likelihood estimator (MLE) and minimum variance unbiased estimator (MVUE) of $\theta$.
